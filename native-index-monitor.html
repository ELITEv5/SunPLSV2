Can SunPLS become PulseChain's native monetary unit?

This page is a live theoretical test — running two new purchasing power oracles alongside the deployed SunPLS protocol to see if they could work together one day. No code has changed. Nothing is wired into the controller yet. We are just watching the data.

What is SunPLS

SunPLS is a WPLS-backed CDP protocol on PulseChain. Lock WPLS as collateral, mint SunPLS. A stability fee and redemption mechanism keeps the market price close to the redemption price R — a target the market is expected to chase, not the other way around. The protocol is live, immutable, and fully on-chain.

Oracle 1 — Native Asset Basket Index

Tracks a basket of PulseChain-native assets denominated in WPLS. Starts at 1.0× and is capped at 1% movement per update — slow and deliberate. Every price comes from PulseX V1 on-chain liquidity. No Chainlink. No external feeds. The chain prices itself.

Oracle 2 — Functional Index v2 (calibrated trade sizes)

Measures three economic functions on PulseChain: blockspace cost (20%), market access slippage (40%), and liquidity depth (40%). Trade sizes are derived from live WPLS pool reserves at deploy time, targeting ~0.5% initial slippage: PLSX/WPLS ≈ 509M WPLS, INC/WPLS ≈ 155M WPLS. Collateral access (SunPLS/WPLS) was removed — it was circular. All three components respond to real pool conditions. Runs as the raw-blockspace reference alongside the EMA version.

Oracle 3 — Functional Index v3 (EMA blockspace)

Fixes v2's blockspace volatility. Live data showed gas swings dominating the accepted index (oscillating 0.9872x ↔ 1.0100x from gas alone). v3 applies a 10% EMA to the gas price:

smoothedGas = 0.10 × block.basefee + 0.90 × prevSmoothedGas

A single gas spike contributes only 10% to the smoothed value. Sustained high gas builds up over ~10 updates before reaching 65% of its full effect. The gasSnapshot() function exposes both smoothed and raw gas side-by-side so the monitor can show exactly how much the EMA is dampening vs what raw gas would give. Same weights as v2.

A separate health overlay reads the live vault, stability pool, and price oracle. It can slow or freeze index influence on monetary policy — but it never directly raises R.

The Hypothesis

What if R tracked functional purchasing power? As PulseChain grows — more chain usage, deeper liquidity, rising gas demand — the index rises and R rises with it. SunPLS holders would not hold a static peg. They would hold a claim on PulseChain's own economic function, appreciating slowly as the ecosystem expands.

This is fundamentally different from every stablecoin today. Not pegged to USD. Not pegged to gold. Not a token index. Pegged to measurable economic activity on the chain it lives on — and still fully collateralised and redeemable at all times.

What we're watching

  • Do both indices move in ways that are economically meaningful — tracking real PulseChain conditions rather than noise?
  • Do the two indices agree or diverge? Persistent divergence tells us something important about what each one actually measures.
  • Does the P / Native R ratio stay close to 100%? That would mean the market would naturally find equilibrium under this regime.
  • Does the functional index give different information than the asset basket — or is it just a noisier version of the same signal?
  • Are borrowers and holders not destabilised by slow, capped index movement?

What success looks like

Over weeks and months both indices drift slowly and meaningfully. P / Native R stays close to par. The functional index provides signal that the asset basket misses. The health overlay behaves correctly under stress. If that holds, Controller v2 wires one or both indices in as a parallel deploy alongside the live protocol. SunPLS becomes the first CDP asset with a monetary policy set by the chain it lives on — not by a dollar peg, not by token prices, but by what the chain can do.
